In the previous lesson, we explored Nested Patterns. Now we are going to take a look at Guards, which is an important concept in Krypton. As you may already be aware, many programming languages have some way of talking about adding an extra boolean condition to a pattern match arm, and Krypton is no exception to that general pattern. It is worth mentioning, however, that the exact spelling still matters, because small syntax choices tend to shape how comfortably a reader can move through later code.

The example below demonstrates that a guard appears after the pattern as `if ...`, and it refines when that arm may be chosen. Note that this is helpful when the shape of the data is not quite enough and a small value-level check should stay attached to the same branch. This may sound somewhat ordinary, and in a sense it is, but that is also why it is useful to see it written out plainly before the lessons get more layered. Interestingly, this is somewhat similar to guard clauses in pattern matching languages, although the Krypton version has its own punctuation and naming choices that should be read carefully.
